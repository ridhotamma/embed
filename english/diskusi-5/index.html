Professional Business Communication: Types and Applications

1. Request Letter

As discussed in "Business Communication: Process and Product" (Guffey & Loewy, 2018), request letters should be clear, specific, and courteous while clearly stating the desired action and any relevant deadlines.

Example Situation:

From: Environmental Research Organization

To: Local Business Council

Context: An environmental research organization writing to request participation in a sustainable business practices study, following Guffey's principles of clarity and mutual benefit.

2. Complaint Letter

According to "Excellence in Business Communication" (Thill & Bovée, 2017), effective complaint letters should be factual, objective, and solution-oriented while maintaining professional courtesy.

Example Situation:

From: Healthcare Facility Administrator

To: Medical Equipment Supplier

Context: Following Thill & Bovée's guidelines, a healthcare administrator documents specific equipment issues while maintaining a constructive tone and suggesting practical solutions.

3. Thank You Letter

Locker and Kienzler's "Business and Administrative Communication" (2021) emphasizes that thank you letters serve as crucial tools for maintaining professional relationships and should be specific, timely, and sincere.

Example Situation:

From: NGO Director

To: Corporate Sponsor

Context: Following Locker and Kienzler's recommendations, the letter specifically details project outcomes and impact while expressing genuine appreciation for the sponsor's support.
